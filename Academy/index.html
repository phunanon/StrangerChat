De-escalation It is possible to turn down the heat in some conversations, and at the very least not add fuel to the fire. Methods of de-escalating another member:
  • Step in early, calmly
  • If you think the member knows they're in the wrong, use indirect moderation with "Enforce rule"
  • Try to have the conversation in a ticket if possible - reduces audience
  • Stay calm and neutral; avoid matching their agitated tone or emotion
  • Acknowledge their frustration (“I understand this is upsetting”) without agreeing or blaming
  • If they are complaining about another server member, assuring them that the matter has been noted, and can be further investigated by all staff
  • Deferring to a higher staff member - the member might be willing to calmly wait for another staff member's opinion
In order to not escalate a situation, do not:
  • Respond with sarcasm, mockery, minimisation, or visible irritation
  • Publicly argue
  • Bringing up unrelated past issues
  • Using accusatory or loaded language (“Youâ€™re overreacting”)
  • Issue sudden warnings without clear prior communication
  • Reply along with multiple staff at once
  • Taking sides in disputes instead of staying neutral
Remember to think to yourself when you see a conversation become heated: “I am no longer a regular server member - I am staff.” You should not return to a regular member mindset until the matter is resolved.
